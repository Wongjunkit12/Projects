Capital Flow of the World

Capital Flow is the movement of money for the purpose of trade and business operations between international entities. Trading forms the backbone of many countries' economies, allowing them to access goods and services not produced domestically. This dashboard provide insights in the dynamics of capital flow between countries globally.

Balance of Trade of Countries (2000 - 2020)

The trade balance is the difference between the value of a country's exports and imports. A positive trade balance means that a country exports more than it imports, while a negative trade balance imples the opposite. Through this map, we can observe the distribution of trade balances across the world and analyse the trends and patterns of global trade. Due to the 2008 global financial crisis, a lot of countries suffered a decline in trade in 2009 as their economies struggled to recover.

Hover over annotations to see their tooltips.

Trade Volume of Countries (2000 - 2020)

The total trade volume is the sum of a countries' imports and exports. The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the total value of goods and services produced by a country in a year. A large trading volume implies a healthy economy as it indicates that a country is actively engaged in the global market and has a high demand and supply of goods and services. A large trading volume can also foster economic growth, innovation and cooperation among countries, boosting it's GDP.

Hover over annotations to see their tooltips.

Top N Countries with Most Exports (2000 - 2020)

Exports are goods or services produced in one country that are sold to another country. Exports are important for a country's economy, as they can create jobs, increase incomes, foster innovation and more. Through this bar chart, it can be deduced easily which countries are the top exporters of the world. In addition, one can observe how the exports of different countries vary over time, influenced by external factors like the 2008 global financial crisis or COVID-19.
